My kingdom for a
saltwater fish!

Healthy, thriving, well-stocked saltwater aquariums are amazing sights to behold. People are irresistibly drawn to stare into them and visually explore every corner, cave, and surface. From the vibrant, striking color palettes of the soft and stony corals to the fascinating behaviors and mesmerizing motions or even the incredible abundance and diversity of life, a saltwater aquarium is a feast for the eyes. The center stage in any aquarium, however, belongs to the fish.

Choosing the right fish for their first saltwater aquarium can seem like a daunting task for a beginning saltwater aquarium hobbyist. There are a number of factors that need to be considered when planning how to stock the fish in a new tank. Diet, feeding habits, aggression, shyness, minimum tank size, territory requirements, water parameters, and size when full grown are just some of those. Trying to match compatible fish can feel overwhelming at first, but it really isn't. This is a well established hobby with countless practitioners who are more than eager to share their wealth of knowledge with anyone just bitten by the saltwater bug. That is the purpose of this website - to simplify the decision making process by profiling seven of the most common, colorful, interesting, inexpensive, and beginner friendly saltwater fish available. They are the following:

  1. Clownfish
  2. Firefish
  3. Royal Grammas
  4. Dwarf Angelfish
  5. Green Chromis
  6. Yellowtail Damselfish
  7. Six Line Wrasses
